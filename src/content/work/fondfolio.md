Fondfolio is a forever project—an evolving product that has grown slowly over the years. It began as an MVP, as simple as a SurveyMonkey URL and a spiral-bound book printed at Staples for one of our best friends' 30th birthday. We gave it to him at a bar with all his friends, and while nothing glamorous, it was easy to see even at this early stage that we tapped into something special, something emotional, something meaningful.

At its core, Fondfolio elevates this same initial concept, but makes it easier, polished and accessible. Our goal was to capitalize on the inside words that are often left un-spoken, and provide an outlet for us to share all the ways that individuals have positively influenced us. Too often, we lack the reminders or triggers to say these things to one another—at least not until it's too late.

The first attempt to formalize Fondfolio as a digital product involved a simple Node.js server with a React and Redux frontend. The project gained momentum when Next.js was released, enabling me to build the first proper iteration that we launched in 2015.

Fondfolio has been my playground for growth and experimentation as a developer. It has given me the space to learn, and forcing function to understand all the moving parts of complex applications. I've been fortunate to work within strong technical ecosystems, and there is a bi-directional influence between my job-work and my personal-work.

Fast forward to today, and the stack has evolved again. While I remain with Next.js somewhat begrudgingly, I've moved toward simpler, more sustainable choices. I've abandoned styled-components in favor of straightforward CSS and only rely on dependencies where necessary. My goal has been to keep things minimal, lightweight, scalable, and built for longevity.

On the UI side, I've been moving away from a component library and trends and towards a more minimalist approach. This is currently being rolled out in phases with the goal of eventually moving to a more modular architecture where Authentication, contributions, and database interactions are now neatly structured within a clean monorepo.

There is no final form to Fondfolio. It is an ever-growing, ever-evolving project, just like the web itself.
